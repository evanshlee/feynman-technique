## Round 2

**Explanation:**
> OK. Specific signal: Marc Andreessen's test – if you took your product away, would customers be upset? If yes, you have fit. Also: organic growth. Customers tell friends without you paying for marketing. And retention – people don't churn after using it. Without fit, you're pushing the rock uphill, constantly selling. With fit, customers pull it from you.


## Round 3

**Explanation:**
> Good push. Product-market fit is "the thing works for people." Product-channel fit is "we can reach those people profitably." You can have PMF but no viable channel: your product solves a real pain, but CAC is higher than LTV because you can't scale the acquisition. Or the inverse: great channel, mediocre product, customers churn. You need both, but PMF comes first because without it, pouring money into a channel just accelerates bleeding.

## Refined Explanation

Product-market fit describes the state where a product delivers enough value that the market pulls it from the company rather than the company pushing it to the market. Observable signals include: customers would be upset if the product disappeared (Andreessen's take-away test), organic referral growth without paid marketing, and high retention without active re-selling. It differs from product-channel fit, which is about whether you can reach customers profitably — a company can achieve PMF without having a viable channel (CAC > LTV), or have strong channels but mediocre retention. PMF must come first because investing in a channel without PMF simply accelerates churn-driven losses.
